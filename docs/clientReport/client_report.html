How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

Since it was a website we had to develop, I wanted to try something I hadn’t done before—something that involved more animations and interactive elements. I had everything planned out until they told us we would be working with developers on our idea. I adjusted the plan slightly but kept the original concept, mainly searching for websites that could help with animations.

How did you communicate with your developer? Were there any issues with communication?

We only communicated during class sessions. It would have been better to share another way to stay in touch—ideally not just email, but also a phone number or a social media account.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

From one of the reports he gave me, I could see he understood the idea I had in mind. I haven’t seen the final version of the site yet—maybe I will soon. That said, I'm already late turning in this report, so I have to move forward with what I know.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

This was my first time participating in something like this, but next time I would work much more closely with the developer. From day one, they should have access to the site link so I can monitor changes as they happen—not just at the very end.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

In terms of developing the site itself, I didn’t really run into problems. Overall, I wouldn't say I had any major issues, but the experience could have been better if everyone had been more excited about working as a team.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I tried to push myself a little by using JavaScript and giving the page some custom animation. Achieving that and realizing it wasn’t as hard as I thought felt really rewarding.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I’d get to know my team better—grab their Instagram, make a group chat or something like that to have a more direct and fun way of communicating.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

Yes. Besides this project, I also worked on another one using the same tools. This experience helped me realize what I could improve and which tools are more effective for projects like these. In that other project, I applied them more successfully—mostly because the team’s collaboration was stronger. Their involvement made a big difference.
